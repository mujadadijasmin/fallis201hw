How to Survive Winter in Boston

Pastel skies, snowflakes, and freezing sidewalks.

1. Dress for Real Boston Weather
  • Get a down coat that blocks wind coming off the harbor.
  • Layer: base + fleece or wool + coat.
  • Waterproof boots = warm feet, less sadness.
  • Always keep hat, scarf, and mittens in your bag.
  • Thermal socks > thin cotton socks.
2. Make Your Apartment Cozy
  • Insulate drafty windows with plastic kits.
  • Soft rugs = warmer floors.
  • Cute blankets and hot tea fix almost anything.
  • Close curtains at night to keep heat in.
  • Know who to call if your heat stops working.
3. Walk Like a Local
  • Check the weather + MBTA before leaving.
  • On ice: smaller steps, no rushing.
  • Carry dry socks on slushy days.
  • Extra commute time is normal during storms.
  • A coat with a hood saves your hair on surprise snow days.
4. Take Care of Your Body
  • Heated air dries your skin → drink water.
  • Lip balm + moisturizer = winter essentials.
  • Warm food helps (soup, ramen, oatmeal).
  • Get some daylight each day.
  • Vitamin D might help — talk to a doctor first.
5. Protect Your Mood
  • Plan tiny weekly joys: coffee, movies, walks.
  • Call your family, text your friends.
  • Create a cozy corner for reading or shows.
  • Stretch or do light exercise inside.
  • Talk to a counselor if winter feels heavy.
6. Know When It’s Serious
  • Learn signs of hypothermia and frostbite.
  • Check wind chill — “feels like” matters.
  • Read about Boston’s winter climate.
  • If you drive: keep a small emergency kit.
  • Spring feels magical when you survive a Boston winter.
